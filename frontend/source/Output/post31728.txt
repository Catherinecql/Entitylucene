You put them in your library, and click sync. If you have duplicates in your library, you will have duplicates on your device. What you see listed in iTunes is exactly what you will have on your device.  The answer is not any harder than this. "Sync" means exactly what it says on the tin, it updates your device with the data in your library. It will "update" songs that have had information changed (cover art, lyrics, or if a title was corrected, etc.), and it will add new ones you've put in the iTunes library.  When you click sync, iTunes does not overwrite everything and start from scratch. It emsynchronizes/em your library. It picks up new changes and writes them to the device, and it will even bring new content emback/em from the device if you bought something from the store on your device.
